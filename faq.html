Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about how values work on this site and how to trade safely.

Where do these values come from?

Values are estimated from community trading activity, rarity, and how obtainable an item currently is. They're a reference point, not a guaranteed price — actual trades can go higher or lower depending on demand at the time.

Why did an item's value change?

Values shift when items are discontinued, when a new case or bundle is released, or when community demand changes. Check the Value Changes page for a running log with reasons.

Is the trade calculator official?

No — it's an independent tool that adds up tracked values on each side of a trade and flags whether it looks fair, an overpay, or an underpay based on a small percentage threshold. Use it as a gut check, not a guarantee.

How do I actually trade in-game?

Trading happens entirely inside Murder Duels through its in-game trading system. This site doesn't process trades — it's only a values reference and a place to post trade ads.

How do I avoid getting scammed?

Only trade through the official in-game trade window, double-check every item on both sides before confirming, and be cautious of anyone rushing you, asking you to trade outside the game, or offering deals that look too good to be true.

Is this site affiliated with Murder Duels or Roblox?

No. This is an independent, fan-made resource built by players, for players. It isn't affiliated with or endorsed by the developers of Murder Duels or by Roblox Corporation.
